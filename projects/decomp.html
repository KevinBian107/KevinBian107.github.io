DECOMP: Cross-region Shared Subspaces in Mouse Cortex

📅 Project Timeline: 2026
Website GitHub

Movement and locomotion variables are decodable from nearly every region of the mouse brain, but this ubiquity leaves a deeper question open: do distant regions share a single low-dimensional global state inherited along the same neural axes, or do they independently develop region-specific geometries whose only commonality is that the same scalar can be read from each? DECOMP reframes this as a question about geometry rather than decodability — same information, different geometry — and asks how much of the cross-region shared subspace between mouse visual cortex (V1), motor cortex (M1), and cerebellum (CB) reflects a common population code versus parallel codes that happen to carry the same behavioral signal.

Across 15 simultaneous dual-region recordings from the IBL Brain-Wide Map, we built a multi-stage pipeline: ridge-regression GLMs with raised-cosine kernels for per-neuron movement variance, shared variance component analysis (SVCA) for split-half-reliable within-region summaries, partial CCA with Frisch–Waugh–Lovell partialling to remove rich behavioral covariates (wheel, pupil, whisker, body motion, lick rate), and lag plus peri-event analyses to characterize the temporal structure of what survives.

All three region pairs retain 0.69–0.85 of their cross-region shared geometry after behavioral partialling, but the surviving structure means different things in each pair. V1↔CB shows zero-lag coupling with flat task responses, consistent with an unmeasured global brain state; V1↔M1 shows zero-lag but strongly task-locked responses, consistent with a shared cognitive state without directional transmission; CB↔M1 shows a ~40ms cerebellar lead with motor-event locking, consistent with genuine cerebellothalamocortical communication. Scalar survival ratios under partialling are necessary but not sufficient — temporal dynamics expose three distinct biological mechanisms hidden behind nearly identical proportional statistics.
