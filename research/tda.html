Computational Neural Topology

We like to talk about things in Euclidean space, but most of the times it's extremely complicated trying to find the correct "metric" of understanding things in such space. Under such nice and structured space, we don't know how to talk about complex data and what they are representing. However, as a “coordinate-free” method, computational topology provides a way to understand the shape of data in a more abstract way.

I am currently using computational topological method in Prof. Yusu Wang's DSC 214 to try to understand what's truely happening inside the weight space of neural network when they are training upon specific tasks. Particularly, I am interested in seeing how the VNL system may evolve different topological characteristics across training. Hence, both helping us to better understand what is truly happening inside neural network and as well as providing another metric of comparing with experimental neuronal data (~homeomorphic).
