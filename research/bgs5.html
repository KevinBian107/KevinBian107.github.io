Efficient Deep SSMs for Long Horizon Naturalistic Behavior Generation

I am applying Deep State-Space Models (SSM) - S4 (break down version article of S4) and S5 - to latent data and policy generated by Mimic-MJX for biomechanically realistic behavior sequence dynamics modeling and generation in professor Scott Linderman's lab at Stanford University.

Since naturalistic behaviors are, in nature, to be long horizon, I am building upon previous works in the lab (S5) by framing this sequence modeling problem as an long horizon problem, trying to predict the best next action give the current state given a trajectory that evolves over a long period. We aim to build an naturalistic behavior generative model that is efficient to train and capable of modeling long horizon data.
