Nature of Information Processing & Sequential Decision Making That Makes "Intelligence"

I believe that, no matter for us or for the machines, there exist, in nature, certain "correct" ways of information processing and decision making mechanism that makes "intellience". It just happens that the random search conducted by evolution found such mechanism and presented in the form of our brain.

Inspired by professor Pereira from the Salk Institute: the nature has creative solutions to match objective functions caused by evolution and there exists a strong coupling between such natural behavior and the underlying neural algorithm. Evolution is like a random search that takes a long time, but it delivers a solution that works. The neural underpinning and formation of neural mechanisms happens to be the information processing theory and decision making theory that works because it helps survival to pass on the genes. Ethology is an integration of all computations done from sensory to supervisory signals. A great deal of neuronal mechanism is about our output, our actions, and our interaction with the environment: it is about what we do.

How we theorize these mechanisms may be limited to our imagination and natural mechanisms are not necessarily bounded within these constraints, they often rely on more complex features that only imperfectly map onto human-interpretable categories. I think that using the computational model with the right layer of abstractions would go beyond the limits of human-interpretable labels and potentially establish find natures of how information are processed and decisions are made, hence, benifiting both community of machine learning and neurosciecne.
